PART 01: AI CREATIVE WORK SIMULATOR

Prompt Factory: A Simulator of Creative Labor

How many prompts does it take to lose your imagination?

The promise of Generative AI was to liberate creative labor. Yet in many contemporary workflows, designers increasingly function as operators of algorithmic systems rather than authors of original ideas.

Creative work is gradually shifting from making to prompting, from crafting to selecting, and from designing to endlessly refining machine-generated outputs. Under the logic of productivity and optimization, creativity risks becoming a repetitive cycle of prompt engineering, evaluation, and revision.

This project is a critical design simulation that models the everyday experience of creative workers in AI-driven production environments. Through gameplay, it explores how automation reshapes authorship, agency, and the psychological condition of creative labor.

Key Research Questions

  • • Q1 When creativity becomes prompt engineering, who can still claim authorship?
  • • Q2 Can a game system make players experience the invisible exhaustion of automated creative labor?
  • • Q3 What happens when productivity becomes the only measure of creative value?

PART 01: AI CREATIVE WORK SIMULATOR

"The Rework Hell of Technological Acceleration"

As Generative AI reshapes creative industries, creators are increasingly shifting from makers to optimizers. As generation becomes easier, creative work is reduced to an endless cycle of selection, revision, and regeneration.

Based on research involving 150 creative practitioners, this project develops an unwinnable mathematical model in which each cycle of rework increases approval rates while reducing the player's Thinking Index. Players eventually reach a state of maximum productivity but minimum creativity.

Theoretical Framework: Social Acceleration

Technological progress does not reduce labor; it accelerates expectations of productivity. This project visualizes how AI-driven workflows can transform creativity into an endless optimization loop.

Three Outcomes of Creative Alienation

Players arrive at three distinct endings shaped by their responses to acceleration, optimization, and automation. None represents victory. Each reflects a different condition of creative labor in the age of AI.

ENDING 01 SOBER RESISTANCE

The Resister

The player chooses to leave the system. Rather than surrendering creativity to endless optimization, they preserve their autonomy through refusal. Not a victory, but an act of resistance.

ENDING 02 QUIET BURNOUT

Hollow Man

After prolonged AI-assisted production and passive feedback cycles, the player's Thinking Index reaches zero. A state of Quiet Burnout is triggered. The body continues working, but the creative self is gone.

ENVIRONMENTAL STORYTELLING

Reconstructing Memory Through Interior Space

The pyramid interior combines ancient Egyptian murals, hieroglyphic inscriptions, ceremonial architecture, and contemporary urban relics into a single spatial narrative. Each object functions not only as environmental decoration, but also as a fragment of cultural memory embedded within the scene.

Rather than recreating history, the environment stages a dialogue between ancient mythology and speculative futures. Traditional symbols are intentionally relocated into futuristic architecture, producing a sense of temporal dislocation.

PART 02: RED LINE — 物物相生，记忆交织

RED LINE

Generative Weaving of Cultural Memory

Conventional heritage restoration seeks to recover the original physical form of artifacts. This project explores an alternative possibility:

Can cultural memory survive after the artifact itself has disappeared?

Built in TouchDesigner, the system transforms fragmented 3D-scanned artifacts into dynamic data nodes connected through an evolving generative network. These fragments are continuously rewoven into emergent digital life forms.

In this context, restoration is no longer about reconstructing the past, but enabling memory to persist through new media.

Key Research Questions

Q1Where does memory reside when the artifact is gone?

Q2Is AI weaving history itself, or an alternative possibility of history?

Red Thread & Point Cloud Generative System

Built with TouchDesigner SOP and CHOP networks, the system transforms cultural artifacts into dynamic point-cloud structures connected through evolving red-thread networks.

Users can manipulate particle density, noise force, and connection distance in real time, observing how digital memory continuously emerges and dissolves between order and chaos.

PART 02: RED LINE — 参数化噪波力场与线约束生成 Page 09 / TouchDesigner Generative Forces
TD PARAMETERS

Weaving Fragmented Histories Through Force Fields

The system is built using TouchDesigner SOP networks. Proximity-based connections are first established through Connect SOP, after which Turbulence Noise fields continuously reshape the network structure.

As the force amplitude increases, stable connections begin to oscillate, stretch, and reorganize. The red-thread network moves between order and chaos, simulating the fragmentation and reconnection of memory over time.

History is not a static archive, but a network continuously connected, disturbed, and reassembled.

Translating History Through Interaction

Every click, drag, and parameter adjustment reshapes the digital structure of the artifact.

History is no longer presented as a static object for observation. Instead, it is continuously reactivated through interaction between the user and the system. User actions become a medium connecting past and present, transforming cultural memory from preservation into generation. Each interaction reorganizes particles and red-thread connections, allowing cultural artifacts to acquire new forms of digital life.
